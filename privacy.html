2) What data we collect

  • Account data: email address and authentication identifiers/tokens collected when you sign in via our website or the extension.
  • Usage data: features used (such as brainstorm generation, notepad usage, YouTube transcript extraction, and research board clipping), session timestamps, in-product preferences (such as dark mode and language), and extension version.
  • Device data: browser type, browser version, and operating system details collected automatically when you use the extension.
  • Payment data: processed entirely by Stripe. We do not collect, transmit, or store full card numbers, CVVs, or other payment card details.
  • User-generated content: text you enter into the brainstorm prompt, notes you create in the notepad, and text you clip from webpages. This content is stored locally on your device using Chrome's storage API and is transmitted to our server only when you actively use AI-powered features (such as generating ideas, summarising pages, or chatting with transcripts).
  • Web browsing activity: we collect the URL and page title of webpages only when you actively choose to use the "Summarise Page", "Watch Page", "Research Board Clip", or "YouTube Transcript" features. We do not passively monitor, track, or collect your browsing history or activity at any other time.
  • No IP addresses: we do not collect or store IP addresses. (Our infrastructure providers may transiently process IPs to route traffic; we do not access or retain them.)
  • Special-category data: we do not intentionally collect this.
  • Sources: directly from you (when you sign in, type prompts, create notes, or use features) and automatic collection via the extension (device data, session timestamps).

3) How we use your data

  • Providing and operating Brainstorm Buddy: authenticating your account, processing brainstorm prompts via our AI API, generating ideas, summarising content, operating the notepad, and providing customer support.
  • Personalising your experience: storing your preferences (dark mode, language, clip toggle) and restoring your session state.
  • Improving the extension: diagnosing errors, understanding which features are used, and improving performance and reliability.
  • Abuse prevention: detecting and preventing misuse of our service, including enforcing usage limits per plan.
  • Processing payments: facilitating subscription payments through Stripe.
  • Legal and compliance: maintaining records for tax, accounting, and responding to lawful requests from authorities.

4) Legal bases for processing (UK GDPR Art. 6)

  • Provide and operate Brainstorm Buddy — Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)).
  • Personalise and improve; diagnostics; abuse prevention — Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)). You can object at any time (see section 11).
  • Payments via Stripe — Contract and Legitimate interests (fraud prevention).
  • Legal and compliance — Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)).
  • Non-essential analytics/marketing (if introduced) — Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)); you can withdraw at any time.

5) How we handle and store your data

  • Local storage: notes, research board snippets, brainstorm history, and user preferences are stored locally on your device using Chrome's storage.local and storage.sync APIs. This data remains on your device and is not transmitted to our servers unless you actively trigger an AI feature.
  • Server-side processing: when you use AI features (Generate Ideas, Summarise Page, Chat with Transcript, Chat with Notes), your prompt or content is transmitted to our API server (api.brainstormbuddy.co.uk) via HTTPS encryption. The server processes the request, returns the AI response, and does not persistently store the content of your prompts or AI responses beyond what is needed to count usage against your plan limits.
  • Account data: stored on our server in encrypted form for the duration of your account.
  • Encryption: all data transmitted between the extension and our servers uses TLS/HTTPS encryption. Data at rest is protected by access controls and encryption where technically feasible.
  • Access controls: access to personal data on our servers is restricted to authorised personnel only, with audit logging in place.

6) How we share your data

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We share limited data with the following service providers:

  • Stripe (stripe.com): processes subscription payments. Stripe receives payment information you submit at checkout and processes it under its own privacy policy as an independent controller for regulatory and fraud prevention purposes.
  • Render (render.com): hosts our application server and API infrastructure under a data processing agreement.
  • Google/Gmail: used only for support communications that you initiate by emailing us.
  • AI provider: when you use AI features, your prompt text is sent to our AI provider to generate responses. Prompts are not used to train AI models.

All service providers are bound by contractual obligations and may only process your data for our documented purposes. We may also disclose data if required by law, regulation, or valid legal process.

You may request a copy of the relevant safeguards by contacting us.

8) How long we keep your data (retention)

  • Account data: retained while your account is active; deleted within 30 days after account closure, unless longer retention is required by law.
  • Usage/diagnostic data: retained for up to 12 months, then deleted or anonymised.
  • Payment records: retained for 6 years to meet tax, accounting, and legal requirements.
  • Locally stored data: notes, history, and preferences remain on your device until you delete them or uninstall the extension. We do not have access to this data.

We may retain data longer if necessary to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims.

We use only essential technologies required for authentication and security (such as session cookies for sign-in). We do not use analytics or marketing cookies. If we introduce non-essential cookies or tracking technologies in the future, we will obtain your consent first and provide controls to manage your preferences.

10) Security

We apply technical and organisational measures to protect your data, including: encryption of data in transit (TLS/HTTPS), server-side access controls, audit logging, and secure credential storage. No method of transmission or storage is perfectly secure, but we work to protect your data against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or loss.

Under the UK GDPR, you have the right to: access the personal data we hold about you; rectify inaccurate or incomplete data; erase your data (subject to legal retention requirements); restrict processing in certain circumstances; object to processing based on legitimate interests; data portability (receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format); and withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent.
